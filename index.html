  • 1. The Exorcist
    "The Exorcist" is a classic horror movie released in 1973, directed by William Friedkin and based on the novel by William Peter Blatty. The film follows the story of a young girl named Regan MacNeil, who becomes possessed by a demonic entity. Disturbing and unexplained events start to occur, leading her mother, Chris, to seek the help of two priests, Father Karras and Father Merrin, to perform an exorcism to save her daughter's soul.
  • 2. The Conjuring
    "The Conjuring" is a 2013 supernatural horror film directed by James Wan. The movie is based on the real-life investigations of Ed and Lorraine Warren, paranormal investigators and demonologists. It follows the story of a family who moves into a secluded farmhouse in Rhode Island and experiences a series of terrifying and unexplained supernatural events. As the paranormal activities escalate, the family seeks the help of the Warrens, who uncover the dark and malevolent forces at work in the house. "The Conjuring" is known for its effective and suspenseful storytelling, well-crafted scares, and a focus on the Warrens' attempts to help the afflicted family. The film was successful in revitalizing the horror genre and spawned a successful franchise with several sequels and spin-offs.
  • 3. Insidious
    "Insidious" is a 2010 supernatural horror film directed by James Wan, known for his work on "The Conjuring." The movie follows the Lambert family as they move into a new home, only to discover that their son, Dalton, has fallen into a mysterious coma. As they struggle to understand and cure his condition, they realize that supernatural forces are at play. The film delves into the concept of astral projection, where a person's spirit can leave their body, and the dangers that come with it. The Lamberts enlist the help of a psychic and a demonologist to confront the malevolent entities that threaten their family.
  • 4. Evil dead
    "Evil Dead" is a 1981 horror film directed by Sam Raimi. It tells the story of five friends who travel to a remote cabin in the woods, where they accidentally release ancient demonic forces by reading from a mysterious book known as the "Necronomicon Ex-Mortis." As the evil spirits possess and torment each of the friends, they are forced to battle for their lives in a gruesome and terrifying struggle against the supernatural. The film is known for its graphic violence, innovative camera work, and a unique blend of horror and dark humor. "Evil Dead" has achieved cult status in the horror genre and led to a successful franchise, including sequels, a remake, and a television series.
  • 5.The Thing
    "The Thing" is a 1982 science fiction horror film directed by John Carpenter. It is a remake of the 1951 film "The Thing from Another World" and is itself a prequel to the 2011 film of the same name. The story follows a group of researchers in Antarctica who come into contact with a shape-shifting alien organism. The alien can perfectly mimic any living being it encounters, leading to paranoia and mistrust among the group as they struggle to identify who is human and who has been assimilated by the creature. The film is known for its suspenseful atmosphere, groundbreaking practical effects, and its exploration of the fear of isolation and the unknown.
  • 6. Night of the living dead
    "Night of the Living Dead" is a 1968 black-and-white horror film directed by George A. Romero. It is a pioneering work in the zombie genre and has had a significant impact on the horror film industry. The story revolves around a group of people who become trapped in a remote farmhouse while trying to fend off reanimated corpses, often referred to as zombies. The film explores themes of survival, human nature, and societal breakdown as the characters face the relentless onslaught of the undead.
  • 7. Halloween
    "Halloween" is a 1978 horror film directed by John Carpenter. It is considered a classic in the slasher genre and is often credited with popularizing the trope of the relentless masked killer. The film follows the story of Michael Myers, a mentally disturbed individual who escapes from a psychiatric hospital and returns to his hometown on Halloween night. He begins a murderous spree, targeting a babysitter named Laurie Strode. The film is known for its suspenseful atmosphere, haunting score, and iconic antagonist, Michael Myers, who wears a white mask.
  • 8. Hereditary
    "Hereditary" is a 2018 psychological horror film written and directed by Ari Aster. The movie revolves around the Graham family, who begins to unravel dark and disturbing secrets about their ancestry following the death of the family matriarch. As they delve deeper into their family's history, they discover a terrifying and malevolent supernatural presence that has haunted them for generations. "Hereditary" is known for its intense and disturbing imagery, as well as its exploration of themes like grief, mental illness, and the sinister aspects of family dynamics. The film received critical acclaim for its unsettling atmosphere and powerful performances. It has become a significant entry in modern horror cinema, known for its deep psychological horror and emotionally harrowing storytelling.
  • 9. Paranormal activity (2007)
    "Paranormal Activity" is a 2007 found-footage horror film directed by Oren Peli. The story is presented as a series of home videos and security camera footage that documents the haunting of a young couple, Katie and Micah. They become increasingly disturbed by unexplained and supernatural occurrences in their home. The film focuses on the suspenseful buildup of paranormal activity, including strange noises, moving objects, and eerie occurrences that escalate as the couple attempts to capture evidence of the presence haunting them. "Paranormal Activity" is known for its low budget, minimalistic approach, and reliance on tension and suspense, which creates a sense of realism and fear.
  • 10. The Silence Of The Lambs (1991)
    "The Silence of the Lambs" is a 1991 psychological thriller film directed by Jonathan Demme. The movie follows the story of Clarice Starling, a young FBI trainee, who is assigned to interview Dr. Hannibal Lecter, a brilliant but cannibalistic serial killer, in an effort to gain insights that can help solve an ongoing serial murder case. As Clarice delves deeper into her interactions with Dr. Lecter, she becomes involved in a psychological cat-and-mouse game that is both gripping and chilling. She seeks the help of Lecter to profile another serial killer known as "Buffalo Bill," who is on a killing spree.
